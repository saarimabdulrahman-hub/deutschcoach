# A1 · Unit 1 · Lesson 2 — Wie geht's?
### *Sich erkundigen* — Ask how someone is and respond

**Level:** A1 · **Unit:** 1 "Hallo!" · **Lesson:** 2 of 6
**Capability:** *Ask how someone is, respond, and bounce the question back.*
**Duration:** ~9 min · **New items:** 9 · **Grammar pattern:** *wie geht's* + gut/schlecht/danke (no new conjugation)
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 1 (greetings, *ich heiße*, *Tschüss*)
**Spiral review:** Hallo, Tschüss, danke (from L1)

---

## 1. Learning objective
By the end, a complete beginner can:
- **ask** "How are you?" in German (*Wie geht's?*),
- **respond** with *gut*, *es geht*, *nicht so gut*,
- **be polite** with *danke* and bounce the question back (*und dir?*),
- combine it with a greeting to open a real conversation.

## 2. Success criteria
- [x] Completable by a zero-German learner unaided
- [x] Learner can produce *"Hallo! Wie geht's? … Gut, danke. Und dir?"*
- [x] Learner recognises all 9 items in Mini Review
- [x] Learner feels "I can ask how someone is doing"
- [x] Items seeded to SRS

## 3. Dialogue  *A first check-in*

**Scene:** Two friends meet and ask how each other is doing.

| # | Speaker | German | 🔊 | English *(tap)* |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Anna | **Hallo, Ben! Wie geht's?** | HAH-loh · ben · vee gayts | Hi Ben! How are you? |
| 2 | Ben | **Gut, danke. Und dir?** | goot · DAHN-kuh · oont deer | Good, thanks. And you? |
| 3 | Anna | **Es geht. Aber nicht so gut.** | ess gayt · AH-ber · nisht zo goot | It's okay. But not great. |
| 4 | Ben | **Oh, schade. Bis bald!** | oh · SHAH-duh · biss bahlt | Oh, too bad. See you soon! |
| 5 | Anna | **Bis bald, Ben!** | biss bahlt | See you soon, Ben! |

## 4. Vocabulary

### Set 1 — Asking & answering (5 items)
| German | 🔊 | Meaning | Example | Common confusion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wie geht's?** | vee gayts | How are you? (informal) | *Wie geht's?* | Short for *Wie geht es dir?* Only with friends/family. |
| **gut** | goot | good | *Gut, danke.* | Replies *Wie geht's?*. Not "ja" — "gut" is the answer. |
| **es geht** | ess gayt | it's okay / so-so | *Es geht.* | Flat hand gesture: "middling". Don't translate literally. |
| **schlecht** | shlesht | bad | *Nicht so gut.* | *Schlecht* is strong — *nicht so gut* is softer/more common. |
| **danke** | DAHN-kuh | thank you | *Gut, danke.* | Review from L1. Always pair with a reply — *Gut, danke.* |

### Set 2 — Keeping it going (4 items)
| German | 🔊 | Meaning | Example | Common confusion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Und dir?** | oont deer | And you? (informal) | *Gut, danke. Und dir?* | Bounces the question back. *Dir* = "to you". |
| **aber** | AH-ber | but | *Aber nicht so gut.* | Softens bad news. |
| **schade** | SHAH-duh | too bad / pity | *Oh, schade!* | Short sympathetic response. Don't overuse. |
| **Bis bald!** | biss bahlt | See you soon! | *Bis bald!* | More specific than *Tschüss* — implies you'll meet again soon. |

---

## 5. Pattern Discovery — *Wie geht's?* and its replies

**Notice:** In the dialogue Ben replies *"Gut, danke. Und dir?"* — three short words that keep the conversation going.

**Name it: The *Wie geht's?* pattern**
| Question | Reply | Polite add-on | Bounce-back |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wie geht's? | gut / es geht / nicht so gut | danke | Und dir? |

**Why:** *Wie geht's?* expects a short description word (*gut, schlecht*), not a long answer. Add *danke* to be polite. *Und dir?* keeps the conversation moving — it's the social glue.

**Emma (optional):** "Think of it like English 'How's it going?' — you don't give a medical report. Just *gut* or *es geht*. Short, friendly, done."

## 6. Guided Practice (4 exercises)

### Ex 1 — Recognition (MCQ)
- **Prompt:** *"Welches Wort bedeutet 'how'?"* → ( **wie** ) ( wo ) ( wer )
- **Mistake:** confusing *wie* with *wo* (where).
- **Feedback ✓:** "Genau — *wie* means 'how'." ✗ "It's *wie* — like in *Wie geht's?*"

### Ex 2 — Matching (pairs)
- **Match:** *gut · es geht · schade · Bis bald* ↔ *good · it's okay · too bad · see you soon*

### Ex 3 — Ordering
- **Prompt:** put in order → [ geht's ] [ dir ] [ Und ] [ Wie ] → **Wie geht's? Und dir?**

### Ex 4 — Fill-in
- **Prompt:** *"___ geht's?"* → ( Wo ) ( **Wie** ) ( Wer )
- **Feedback ✗:** "Remember the question starts with *Wie* — 'how'."

## Common mistakes
| Mistake | Why | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| *"Ich bin gut"* | Literal "I am good" from English | *Gut* alone or *Mir geht's gut* (later) |
| *"Wie geht's Sie?"* | Mixing formal *Sie* with casual *geht's* | *Wie geht's?* is casual; formal is *Wie geht es Ihnen?* (Unit 1 Lesson 5) |
| Forgetting *danke* | English habits | Always pair reply with *danke* |
| *"Und du?"* instead of *Und dir?* | Nominative for dative | *Dir* after *und* in this phrase — treat as a chunk |
