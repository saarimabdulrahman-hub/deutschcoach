# A1 · Unit 1 · Lesson 5 — Du oder Sie?
### *Höflichkeit* — Use formal and informal German appropriately

**Level:** A1 · **Unit:** 1 · **Lesson:** 5 of 6
**Capability:** *Know when to use du or Sie and switch between them.*
**Duration:** ~9 min · **New items:** 9 · **Grammar:** *du* vs *Sie* register; *Guten Tag / Auf Wiedersehen*
**Spiral:** All prior greetings, verb forms, introductions

---

## 1. Learning objective
Learner can:
- **distinguish** informal (*du*, *Hallo*, *Tschüss*) from formal (*Sie*, *Guten Tag*, *Auf Wiedersehen*),
- **greet formally**: *Guten Tag, ich heiße … Wie heißen Sie?*,
- **say goodbye formally**: *Auf Wiedersehen*,
- choose the right register for the situation.

## 2. Success criteria
- [x] Unaided completion
- [x] Produces *Guten Tag, wie heißen Sie?* and *Auf Wiedersehen*
- [x] Can identify whether a situation needs *du* or *Sie*
- [x] Feels "I know when to be formal"

## 3. Dialogue  *Two contexts — same introduction*

### Scene A — Friends (informal) — review
| 1 | Ben | **Hallo! Ich heiße Ben. Wie heißt du?** |
| 2 | Sofia | **Ich heiße Sofia. Freut mich!** |
| 3 | Ben | **Tschüss, Sofia!** |

### Scene B — At a job interview (formal)
| 1 | Herr Müller | **Guten Tag. Ich heiße Müller. Wie heißen Sie?** | GOO-ten tahk · ish HY-suh MÜL-ler · vee HY-sen zee |
| 2 | Frau Schmidt | **Ich heiße Schmidt. Freut mich.** | ish HY-suh SHMITT · froyt mish |
| 3 | Herr Müller | **Auf Wiedersehen, Frau Schmidt.** | owf VEE-der-zay-en · frow SHMITT |

*Compare: Ben uses **du** + **Tschüss**. Herr Müller uses **Sie** + **Guten Tag** + **Auf Wiedersehen**.*

## 4. Vocabulary

### Set 1 — Formal toolkit (5 items)
| German | 🔊 | Meaning | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Guten Tag** | GOO-ten tahk | Good day / Hello (formal) | Shops, strangers, professionals |
| **Wie heißen Sie?** | vee HY-sen zee | What is your name? (formal) | *heißen* in the Sie-form = *heißen* (same as infinitive) |
| **Auf Wiedersehen** | owf VEE-der-zay-en | Goodbye (formal) | Lit. "on seeing-again" |
| **Herr** | hair | Mr. | *Herr Müller* — always with last name |
| **Frau** | frow | Mrs. / Ms. | *Frau Schmidt* — always with last name |

### Set 2 — The shift (4 items)
| German | 🔊 | Meaning | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **du** | doo | you (informal) | Friends, family, children, young people |
| **Sie** | zee | you (formal) | Strangers, work, officials, older people. ALWAYS capitalised. |
| **hallo** | HAH-loh | hi (informal) | Review — informal only |
| **tschüss** | CHÜSS | bye (informal) | Review — informal only |

**Decision table: *du* or *Sie*?**

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Friend your age | **du** |
| A child | **du** |
| A teacher / boss you don't know well | **Sie** |
| A shop assistant | **Sie** |
| Someone 30 years older | **Sie** (until they offer *du*) |
| Your partner / close friend | **du** |

---

## 5. Pattern Discovery — The *Sie* form

**Notice:** *heißen* in the *Sie*-form: **heißen** — it's the same word as the infinitive, just with *Sie* after.

| Register | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Informal | Wie **heißt** du? | Ben → Sofia |
| Formal | Wie **heißen** Sie? | Herr Müller → Frau Schmidt |

**Why:** German has two "you"s. *Du* is personal — for friends. *Sie* is respectful — for everyone else. When you use *Sie*, the verb keeps the infinitive ending *-en*. Always capitalise *Sie*. Always pair *Guten Tag* with *Sie*.

**Emma:** "Rule of thumb: if you'd use their first name → *du*. If you'd say Mr/Ms → *Sie*. Germans take this seriously — it's actually rude to *du* a stranger. But don't stress — people are kind to learners."

## 6. Guided Practice (4 exercises)

### Ex 1 — Recognition: *du* or *Sie*?
- **Prompt:** *"You meet your friend →"* → ( **du** ) ( Sie )
### Ex 2 — Matching
- **Match** register pairs: *Hallo · du · Tschüss* ↔ *informal* | *Guten Tag · Sie · Auf Wiedersehen* ↔ *formal*
### Ex 3 — Fill-in
- **Prompt:** *"Wie ___ Sie?" (formal)* → ( heißt ) ( **heißen** ) ( heiße )
### Ex 4 — Micro-production
- **Prompt:** *Greet your new boss formally.* → **Guten Tag, ich heiße … . Wie heißen Sie?**
- Tolerant: accept any name; check for *heißen* + *Sie*

## Common mistakes
| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| *Sie* not capitalised | Mid-sentence *Sie* must be capitalised — it's a grammar rule |
| Using *du* with strangers | Default to *Sie* with anyone you don't know |
| *"Guten Tag, Tschüss!"* mixed | Match your greeting and goodbye: formal→formal, informal→informal |
