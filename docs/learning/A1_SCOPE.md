# DeutschFlow — A1 Curriculum Scope & Roadmap

**Version:** 1.0 · **Updated:** 2026-07-12 · **Status:** Active — single source of truth for A1
**Sprint:** 7.1 · **Governed by:** LESSON_BLUEPRINT · CURRICULUM_PRINCIPLES · VOCABULARY/GRAMMAR/SPEAKING/FEEDBACK/AI_TUTOR guidelines · LESSON_PRINCIPLES/FLOW/WIREFRAMES

> This document defines the entire A1 journey. Every A1 lesson is authored against it. It does not contain lessons, exercises, or word lists (those are Sprint 7.2+); it defines **what** is taught, **in what order**, and **why**.

---

## 1. Overall learning goal

After A1, a learner can **survive and connect in everyday German** — handle predictable, concrete situations using simple sentences, at a slow pace, with a patient speaker. This is CEFR A1 ("Breakthrough").

### Measurable outcomes (the A1 exit profile)
A learner who completes A1 can, unaided:

| Skill | Measurable "can-do" |
|---|---|
| **Speaking** | Introduce themselves, ask & answer ~10 personal questions, order food, ask directions, make a simple plan — in short sentences |
| **Listening** | Follow a slow 6–10 line dialogue on a familiar topic and extract key facts (name, price, time, place) |
| **Reading** | Understand short notices, menus, signs, simple messages |
| **Vocabulary** | ~500 high-frequency words active (recognition + basic production) |
| **Grammar** | Present tense (regular, *sein*, *haben*, separable), modal verbs, nominative + accusative, articles/gender/plural, negation, W- & yes/no questions, possessives, imperative |
| **Confidence** | Willingly attempts speaking; treats mistakes as normal |

**Exit test (non-punitive):** the A1 Milestone Review is a **can-do self-affirmation checklist**, not an exam (see §6).

## 2. Lesson roadmap

**~52 lessons across 10 units** (≈44 capability lessons + 8 unit reviews + a final A1 milestone). Each lesson = **one capability**; each unit = a themed, completable chunk (CURRICULUM_PRINCIPLES). "R" = review/checkpoint lesson.

### Unit overview

| Unit | Theme | Core capability (CEFR outcome) | Lessons |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hallo!** First contact | Greet people & say who you are | 6 |
| 2 | **Wer bist du?** Meeting people | Exchange basic personal info | 6 |
| 3 | **Zahlen & Infos** Numbers & info | Give numbers, age, contact details | 6 |
| 4 | **Dinge & Artikel** Things & articles | Name & describe everyday objects | 6 |
| 5 | **Essen & Trinken** Food & drink | Order food and pay | 6 |
| 6 | **Familie & Menschen** Family & people | Talk about family & describe people | 5 |
| 7 | **Tagesablauf & Zeit** Routine & time | Describe your day & tell the time | 6 |
| 8 | **Hobbys & Freizeit** Hobbies | Talk about free time & make plans | 5 |
| 9 | **Einkaufen & Wege** Shopping & directions | Shop & find your way around | 6 |
| 10 | **Zuhause & Alltag** Home / A2 bridge | Describe where you live; first past-tense | 5 |

### Per-unit detail

**Unit 1 — Hallo! (First contact)**
- **Objective:** greet and open a conversation; say your name.
- **Grammar:** `ich heiße / ich bin` (formulaic, verb-second, no case yet); `du` vs `Sie` register.
- **Vocab focus:** greetings, courtesy, the alphabet.
- **Speaking goal:** greet Emma and say your name (1–2 turns).
- **Lessons:** 1) Say hello & goodbye · 2) Ask how someone is · 3) Say your name · 4) Formal vs informal (du/Sie) · 5) Spell your name (Alphabet) · R) Review.

**Unit 2 — Wer bist du? (Meeting people)**
- **Objective:** ask & answer where you're from, what you speak, introduce a third person.
- **Grammar:** verb **sein** (bin/bist/ist…); W-questions (wer/wie/wo/woher); nominative pronouns.
- **Vocab:** countries, nationalities, languages.
- **Speaking:** a short "getting to know you" exchange.
- **Lessons:** 1) Where are you from? · 2) Nationalities & languages · 3) The verb *sein* · 4) W-questions · 5) Introduce someone else · R) Review.

**Unit 3 — Zahlen & Infos (Numbers & info)**
- **Objective:** use numbers for age, phone, prices; ask yes/no questions.
- **Grammar:** verb **haben**; yes/no questions (inversion).
- **Vocab:** numbers 0–100, age, contact details.
- **Speaking:** exchange age & contact info.
- **Lessons:** 1) Numbers 0–20 · 2) Numbers 21–100 · 3) How old are you? · 4) Contact details · 5) The verb *haben* + yes/no questions · R) Review (+ first **mixed** cross-unit review, see §7).

**Unit 4 — Dinge & Artikel (Things & articles)**
- **Objective:** name and describe everyday objects; say what something is / isn't.
- **Grammar:** articles **der/die/das** & **ein/eine**; plural; negation with **kein**.
- **Vocab:** everyday objects, colours.
- **Speaking:** describe objects around you.
- **Lessons:** 1) This is a… (ein/eine) · 2) der/die/das (gender) · 3) Everyday objects · 4) Plurals · 5) Negation with *kein* + colours · R) Review.

**Unit 5 — Essen & Trinken (Food & drink)**
- **Objective:** order food/drink politely and pay.
- **Grammar:** **möchten**; **accusative** (indefinite article) introduced *through ordering*; `gern`.
- **Vocab:** food, drinks, café.
- **Speaking:** order a meal with Emma (roleplay).
- **Lessons:** 1) Food & drink · 2) Ordering (Ich möchte…) · 3) *möchten* · 4) Accusative articles · 5) Prices & paying + likes (gern) · R) Review — *order a meal* checkpoint.

**Unit 6 — Familie & Menschen (Family & people)**
- **Objective:** talk about family and describe people.
- **Grammar:** possessives **mein/dein/sein/ihr** (nom + acc); predicative adjectives.
- **Vocab:** family, character/appearance.
- **Speaking:** describe your family.
- **Lessons:** 1) Family members · 2) mein/dein · 3) sein/ihr/unser · 4) Describing people · R) Review.

**Unit 7 — Tagesablauf & Zeit (Routine & time)**
- **Objective:** describe a daily routine and tell the time.
- **Grammar:** present tense of **regular verbs**; **separable verbs** (aufstehen…); temporal prepositions (um/am).
- **Vocab:** routine verbs, times, days.
- **Speaking:** describe your day.
- **Lessons:** 1) Days & parts of day · 2) Telling the time · 3) Present tense (regular verbs) · 4) Separable verbs · 5) Time expressions · R) Review — *my day* checkpoint (+ mixed review).

**Unit 8 — Hobbys & Freizeit (Hobbies)**
- **Objective:** talk about free time and make simple plans/invitations.
- **Grammar:** **modal verbs** (können, wollen) + the **sentence bracket** (modal … infinitive-final); `gern / nicht gern`.
- **Vocab:** hobbies, sports.
- **Speaking:** talk about hobbies & propose a plan.
- **Lessons:** 1) Hobbies & activities · 2) gern / nicht gern · 3) *können* (ability) · 4) *wollen* + making plans · R) Review.

**Unit 9 — Einkaufen & Wege (Shopping & directions)**
- **Objective:** shop for items and ask for/give directions.
- **Grammar:** accusative consolidation; **imperative**; local prepositions (wo/wohin, light) + **es gibt**.
- **Vocab:** shops, quantities, places in town.
- **Speaking:** ask for & give directions with Emma.
- **Lessons:** 1) Shopping vocabulary · 2) In the shop · 3) Accusative consolidation · 4) Asking directions · 5) Prepositions of place + es gibt / imperative · R) Review — *find your way* checkpoint.

**Unit 10 — Zuhause & Alltag (Home / A2 bridge)**
- **Objective:** describe where you live; understand a longer simple conversation; take first steps into the past.
- **Grammar:** **wohnen** + living vocab; **war/hatte** and a light intro to **Perfekt** of common verbs (bridge to A2 — see §10).
- **Vocab:** home & rooms, weather.
- **Speaking:** describe your home; a short free chat.
- **Lessons:** 1) Home & rooms · 2) Where I live (wohnen) · 3) Weather & small talk · 4) Understanding a conversation (listening consolidation) · M) **A1 Milestone Review** (can-do check).

## 3. Vocabulary targets

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| New words / capability lesson | **8–10** (chunked ≤6) | Working memory; VOCABULARY_GUIDELINES |
| New words / review lesson | 0 (recycles) | Reviews consolidate, don't add load |
| **Total active A1 vocabulary** | **~450–500** | Top ~500 German frequency covers A1 |
| Recognition vs production | ~500 recognized, ~300 producible | Recognition precedes production |

- **High-frequency policy:** A1 draws strictly from the top ~500 lemmas + situationally essential nouns (menu items, family terms). Nouns always taught **with article & plural**.
- **Recycling strategy:** every lesson's **warm-up** pulls 2–3 prior/SRS-due words; every unit's dialogues reuse earlier vocabulary (spiral). Cross-unit review lessons interleave sets.
- **Review cadence:** all lesson vocab seeded to SM-2 on completion; missed items enter at lower ease (surface sooner). See §7.

## 4. Grammar roadmap (sequenced, with rationale)

Principle: **introduce a pattern only when a capability needs it**; simplest/most-frequent first; cases only in concrete contexts; abstractions deferred.

| Order | Pattern | Unit | Why here |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `ich heiße / ich bin` (formulaic V2) | 1 | Usable in minute one; no case needed |
| 2 | du / Sie register | 1 | Social survival before verb tables |
| 3 | **sein** conjugation | 2 | Most frequent verb; needed to describe |
| 4 | W- & yes/no questions | 2–3 | To *interact*, not just state |
| 5 | **haben** | 3 | 2nd most frequent verb; age/possession |
| 6 | Articles der/die/das, ein/eine | 4 | Gender is foundational **but delayed** until learners can already talk (avoids abstract front-loading) |
| 7 | Plural, **kein** | 4 | Extends the noun system + negation |
| 8 | **Accusative** (via *möchten*/ordering) | 5 | Introduced inside a concrete need, not as abstract "case theory" |
| 9 | Possessives (mein/dein…) | 6 | Builds on articles + accusative |
| 10 | Present tense (regular) + separable verbs | 7 | Needed to narrate a routine |
| 11 | **Modal verbs** + sentence bracket | 8 | Ability/plans; introduces verb-final structure |
| 12 | Imperative + local prepositions (light) | 9 | Directions require commands & place words |
| 13 | Past first steps (war/hatte, light Perfekt) | 10 | Bridge to A2 — see §10 |

**Deliberately deferred to A2:** dative case, full Perfekt, comparatives, subordinate clauses (weil/dass), two-way prepositions with case contrast, genitive. Introducing them at A1 would violate cognitive-load limits and confidence-first sequencing.

## 5. Speaking roadmap

- **Begins Lesson 1** — a tiny guided reply (say your name). Early, low-stakes exposure normalizes speaking.
- **Optional always, encouraged always** (Guided-not-Gated). Typed input universally; 🎤 speech later.
- **Confidence ladder:**

| Phase | Units | Speaking task | Scaffold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warm exposure | 1–3 | 1–2 guided turns | Full suggestion chips |
| Guided use | 4–6 | Short roleplay (order, describe) | Suggestions on request |
| Applied | 7–9 | Multi-turn scenario (routine, directions) | Minimal scaffold |
| Free-ish | 10 | Short free chat | Emma prompts |

- **Emma's role:** hint-ladder scaffolding (AI_TUTOR_GUIDELINES) — encourage → hint → explain → reveal; corrects one target thing with a reason; never answers first.
- **Pronunciation feedback:** introduced as **gentle guidance** from ~Unit 3 (when learners produce short phrases) — encouraging, never a score, never a gate. Formal speech scoring is a later dependency; until then Emma gives qualitative pronunciation tips.

## 6. Assessment philosophy

- **Mastery = demonstrated can-do + durable recall**, not a test score.
- **Unit reviews** are *checkpoints* ("order a meal", "find your way") — succeed-and-celebrate, never fail-and-block.
- **SRS retention** is the real assessment of vocabulary (items that stick move to longer intervals).
- **A1 Milestone Review** = a **can-do self-affirmation** ("You can now: greet · introduce yourself · order food · …") plus a light mixed practice — celebratory, non-punitive.
- **No grades, no percentages-as-scores, no gating, no streak-punishment** (FEEDBACK_GUIDELINES, PRODUCT_VISION).

## 7. Review strategy (spaced repetition + mixed practice)

| Mechanism | When | What |
|---|---|---|
| **SRS seed** | Every lesson completion | Vocab → SM-2 (New); missed items lower ease |
| **Warm-up spiral** | Every lesson | 2–3 due/known words reactivated |
| **Unit review lesson** | End of each unit | Interleaves that unit's vocab + pattern; the capability checkpoint |
| **Mixed (cross-unit) review** | From **Unit 3** onward, every ~2 units | Interleaves multiple units' items (interleaving > blocking) |
| **Milestone review** | End of A1 | Broad mixed practice + can-do affirmation |

Review lessons **add no new vocabulary** — they consolidate and build confidence.

## 10. Future compatibility (A1 → A2, no redesign)

- **Same structure:** A2 reuses the identical model — CEFR level → themed units → one-capability lessons → CFCP flow. No engine or curriculum-format change.
- **Explicit bridge:** Unit 10 introduces `war/hatte` + light Perfekt, so A2 opens on the **past tense** without a cold start.
- **Deferred grammar becomes A2's spine:** dative, full Perfekt, subordinate clauses (weil/dass), comparatives, two-way prepositions — already listed as "deferred", so A2 authoring inherits a ready backlog.
- **Vocabulary spirals forward:** A1's ~500 words are the recycled base for A2 (frequency band 500–1500).
- **SRS continuity:** the same deck carries A1 items into A2 review (no reset).

**Result:** A2 planning starts from this document's "deferred" list + the A2 frequency band — a continuation, not a redesign.

## Assumptions & trade-offs
- **Assumption:** ~10 min/day learners; A1 depth is the top content priority (PRODUCT_AUDIT).
- **Trade-off (delaying gender/articles to Unit 4):** learners speak formulaically before mastering gender — an intentional confidence-first choice; gender is then taught *with* every noun thereafter to compensate.
- **Trade-off (accusative via ordering):** teaching a case inside a concrete task risks under-explaining the abstraction; the standalone Grammar reference + Emma cover depth for the curious.
- **Edge case:** true beginners who want a placement/skip path — optional, non-blocking (a Sprint 7.x decision).
