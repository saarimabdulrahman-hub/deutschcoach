# DeutschFlow — Feedback Guidelines

**Version:** 1.0 · **Updated:** 2026-07-12 · **Status:** Active · **Sprint:** 6.3A

> Feedback is the moment learning happens or confidence breaks. It is immediate, specific, kind, and never punitive.

---

## 1. Principles
1. **Immediate** — feedback appears the moment an answer is checked.
2. **Explain the why** — every correction teaches a reason, not just a verdict (PRODUCT_VISION "Explain, don't just drill").
3. **No penalty, no score, no block** — errors adjust *what to review*, never a grade or gate (Guided-not-Gated).
4. **Icon + text, not color alone** — ✓/✗ + words (accessibility; colorblind-safe).
5. **One correction at a time** — fix the target error; ignore noise (cognitive load).
6. **End on a win** — the last thing before celebration is a success (Mini Review).

---

## 2. Patterns (with sample copy)

| Outcome | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Correct** | ✓ affirm + brief why-right | "✓ Genau! *heiße* is the verb after *ich*." |
| **Almost** (tolerant near-miss) | ✓/~ accept + note | "Close enough — note the umlaut: *heiße*." |
| **Incorrect** | ✗ + correct answer + why + (SRS flag, silent) | "Almost — the verb comes second. It's *Ich bin Ben*." |
| **Second miss** | reveal + why + offer Emma | "Here it is: *Ich bin Ben.* Want Emma to explain word order?" |
| **System error** | plain, actionable | "Couldn't save that — we'll retry. Your progress is safe." |

---

## 3. Tolerant matching (typed answers)
Accept when the answer differs only by: case, leading/trailing whitespace, umlaut spelling (ü/ue), trivial punctuation, or a listed `alternate`. Reject only on a genuine content/word-order error. **Bias: avoid false negatives** — a beginner who typed it right but got marked wrong loses trust faster than an occasional lenient pass (which SRS catches).

---

## 4. Errorless vs productive failure
- Early/A1: engineer for success (~80–90% correct) — feedback mostly affirms.
- Later/B1+: allow an attempt to fail *productively*, then feedback teaches — but the safety net (hint → reveal → why) always resolves the struggle. **Never leave a learner in unresolved confusion.**

---

## 5. What feedback must never do
- ❌ Say only "Wrong" / "Incorrect" with no help.
- ❌ Deduct points, show a falling score, or a red "streak lost".
- ❌ Shame ("You should know this"), guilt ("You're behind"), or sarcasm.
- ❌ Rely on color to signal correctness.
- ❌ Block progress on a miss.

## 6. Assumptions & trade-offs
- **Assumption:** feedback strings can be authored per exercise (`why` field) or generated by Emma on demand.
- **Trade-off:** always-kind feedback risks under-signalling severity; we compensate with SRS weighting (missed items return sooner) rather than punitive UX.
- **Edge case:** genuinely ambiguous items → treat as correct if plausibly valid, and improve the item's `alternates`.
